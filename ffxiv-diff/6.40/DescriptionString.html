+++939Fashion Functionality
+++940While much of the equipment in FINAL FANTASY XIV provides advantages in battle, certain pieces are worn purely for the sake of fashion. Mix and match your favorite fashion items to create a style all your own! A special sidequest will unlock customization options allowing you to use these favored pieces to change the appearance of other equipment. Combined with the flexibility of gear sets, you control how you wish to look as you forge ahead on your adventures.
+++941Casting Glamours
+++942Glamours are a means of projecting the appearance of one piece of equipment onto another. This can be accomplished with the subcommand on the item you wish to alter, or by using Cast Glamour from Actions & Traits. Casting glamours requires catalysts known as <UIForeground>F201FA</UIForeground><UIGlow>F201FB</UIGlow>glamour prisms<UIGlow>01</UIGlow><UIForeground>01</UIForeground>, with one prism being consumed per cast. Among other sources, glamour prisms can be obtained from the market board or from Grand Company quartermasters. The presence of a glamour is indicated by a symbol in the upper right corner of the item icon. Removing glamours requires the use of <UIForeground>F201FA</UIForeground><UIGlow>F201FB</UIGlow>glamour dispellers<UIGlow>01</UIGlow><UIForeground>01</UIForeground>, which are also available from market boards or certain vendors. <UIForeground>F201F4</UIForeground><UIGlow>F201F5</UIGlow>※Please note that, for the illusion to take effect, the projected gear must be compatible with the glamoured gear's level, class, race, and gender.<UIGlow>01</UIGlow><UIForeground>01</UIForeground>
+++943Glamour Plates
+++944Created using the glamour dressers found inside inn rooms, glamour plates are a means of projecting an entire ensemble onto your gear. When designing a glamour plate, the equipment you wish to project must be stored inside a glamour dresser or armoire. Ensembles saved to a glamour plate are applied with a single command, and can be projected over and over again without consuming glamour prisms. You can also use the gear set subcommand to link to plates, and instantly don your saved appearance by switching to the linked set.
+++945Glamour Dresser
+++946Glamour dressers are available in every inn room, and can be used to store equipment as glamours. Any type of gear can be stored free of charge, up to the dresser's maximum capacity. The initial transformation consumes one <UIForeground>F201FA</UIForeground><UIGlow>F201FB</UIGlow>glamour prism<UIGlow>01</UIGlow><UIForeground>01</UIForeground> per item, but once glamoured gear has been added to a glamour plate, it can be projected onto your equipment as many times as you like at no cost.
+++947Armoire
+++948Available in inn rooms or placed as furnishings in player estates, armoires are used to store artifact armor, seasonal event costumes, achievement rewards, bonus items, and other specific gear. An unlimited amount of equipment can be stored in the armoire at no cost, but please note that any dyes, crests, or glamours will be removed. Additionally, spiritbond will be reset to 0% and durability will be set to 100%. If you wish to keep dyes when storing items, or wish to use dyed items to cast glamours, the glamour dresser is the more suitable choice.
+++949Dyeing Equipment
+++950You can change the coloring of various gear and furnishing items through the use of <UIForeground>F201FA</UIForeground><UIGlow>F201FB</UIGlow>dyes<UIGlow>01</UIGlow><UIForeground>01</UIForeground>, with one pot of dye being consumed for each distinct section of an item. Should you wish to restore gear to its original hue, a pot of terebinth removes the dye from a single item. Both dyes and terebinth are available from vendors and the market boards, and can also be crafted by Disciples of the Hand from level 30 onwards. Please note that not every piece of equipment is a candidate for dyeing. Items you can dye are marked with the corresponding symbol in the top right corner of their icons.
